My Tech Goals for the Next 2 Years

Welcome to my personal roadmap in the tech field. Here are my goals and plans to achieve them.

Over the next two years, my goals focus on expanding my technical expertise, contributing to the community, and developing leadership skills. These goals are designed to make me a well-rounded professional capable of tackling diverse challenges in the tech industry. By achieving these goals, I aim to position myself at the forefront of technological innovation and make meaningful contributions to the field. I would list my goals and give a better explanation below.

1. Become Proficient in Front-end Development

Becoming proficient in front-end development is a crucial step for anyone aiming to excel in the tech industry. I aim to master frontend technologies, focusing on HTML, CSS JavaScript and React. Front-end development is in high demand. As the digital world continues to expand, businesses across all sectors are increasingly investing in their online presence. This means a growing need for skilled front-end developers who can create engaging, user-friendly websites and applications. Proficiency in front-end development opens doors to numerous career opportunities, from working in tech startups to large corporations, and also freelance work. Front-end developers play a pivotal role in shaping the user experience (UX). They are responsible for creating the look and feel of a website or application, ensuring it is intuitive, responsive, and visually appealing. A well-designed user interface (UI) can significantly enhance user satisfaction and retention, which is critical for the success of any digital product. By mastering front-end development, you can directly impact how users interact with and perceive a product. A strong portfolio is essential for showcasing your skills and attracting potential employers or clients. Proficiency in front-end development allows you to create impressive, functional websites and applications that can demonstrate your capabilities. A well-rounded portfolio featuring various projects can significantly boost your credibility and marketability in the job market.

2. Contribute to Open Source Projects

Engaging with the open-source community will help me improve my coding skills and collaborate with other developers. Working on open-source projects allows me to apply my skills in real-world scenarios. I would gain hands-on experience with actual codebases, which can be far more instructive than theoretical learning or small-scale personal projects. Moreover, based on problem solving, I encounter and solve various technical challenges, which enhances my problem-solving skills. This experience is invaluable as it mirrors the kind of issues I might face in professional development environments. Contributions to open-source projects serve as tangible proof of my skills and experience. They are excellent additions to my portfolio, showcasing my ability to work on real-world projects and collaborate with other developers.

3. Build and Deploy My Own Projects

I plan to create and launch at least three personal projects to enhance my portfolio and practical experience. Deploying my own projects gives me hands-on experience with deployment processes and tools. I would learn how to set up servers, manage databases, and ensure your application is accessible to users. Seeing my project come to life and being used by others provides a great sense of accomplishment. This validation of my skills and efforts can be highly motivating and encourage me to take on more complex projects.

4. Learn Cloud Computing

By learning cloud computing, I would gain a better understanding of how the back-end infrastructure operates. This knowledge helps me to collaborate more effectively with back-end developers, leading to smoother integrations and more cohesive project development. Cloud computing services often include CDNs, which would distribute my content across multiple servers globally. This ensures faster load times and better performance for my front-end applications, especially for users in different geographic zones and locations. Learning about serverless architecture enables me to deploy functions and services without managing servers. This simplifies the deployment process and reduces operational overhead, allowing me to focus more on the front-end aspects of my projects.
